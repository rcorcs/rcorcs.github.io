Rodrigo Rocha

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at The University of Edinburgh.
My research is focused on optimising compilers and supervised by Hugh Leather.
Fundings by the EPSRC Centre for Doctoral Training (CDT) in Pervasive Parallelism and the Institute for Computing Systems Architecture (ICSA).

I received a MSc by Research degree (with distinction) from the School of Informatics, University of Edinburgh, as part of the CDT program in Pervasive Parallelism, which is a MScR + PhD (1 year + 3 years) programme. My Master's thesis is entitled 'Online Iterative Compilation Guided by Work-based Profiling'.

I also received a MSc in Computer Science from the Federal University of Minas Gerais (UFMG), Brazil, in 2015, and my BSc degree (summa cum laude - top grade) in Computer Science from PUC Minas, Brazil, in 2012.
I had a 1-year teaching experience as a Lecturer (Assistant Professor) at PUC Minas, Brazil, for undergraduate courses of Computer Science and Information Systems.
In addition to my research on compilers, I am also a collaborator to the open-source LLVM compiler infrastructure. For me, making research innovations accessible to the wider world is as important as developing them in the first place.

Description [show]

For my Master's dissertation I developed an evolution of the Watershed framework, which is a distributed stream processing system for large-scale data streams, inspired in the data-flow model. We designed a modular abstraction for the stream channels, allowing for reusable and programmable streams. The Watershed-ng has been integrated with the Hadoop ecosystem, including the Hadoop YARN, for job scheduling and cluster resource management; the Hadoop Distributed File System (HDFS); and the ZooKeeper, for distributed coordination.
